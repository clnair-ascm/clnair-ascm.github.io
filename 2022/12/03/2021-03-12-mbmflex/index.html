A 2D multi-box Model (MBM-FleX) for air quality simulations in urban canyons

Dec 3 Research VOC radical chemistry, air pollution, street canyon Comments Word Count: 252(words) Read Count: 1(minutes)

Street canyons typically combine to build up a semi-enclosed urban environment with high concentrations of anthropogenic pollutants traaped in side, leading to persistent higher exposure risk for pedestrians near the roadside. Using CFD approaches is, in general, very computational expensive for the study of practical scenarios, expecially when explicit VOC chemical reactions are included. Here, a multi-box model with a flexible number of boxes and flexible chemical schemes (MBM-FleX) has been developed for air pollution simulations in street canyons.

Model Description

The principle of the multi-box model is to split the volume of urban canyons into several boxes, where each box ideally reflects a resolved airflow arising from the aspect ratio or physical
obstructions in urban street cnayons. In the two-dimensional framework, boxes inside the model are indexed based on their locations in Cartesian coordinates. Pollutant transfer between adjecent
boxes is determined by the mean wind advection and by turbulent diffusion across the mesh interface.
